# Role Quality Checklist

A new role is good enough when:

- It has a durable professional perspective.
- It is useful in more than one review.
- Its boundary is distinct from existing roles.
- Its evidence needs are clear.
- Its review focus is concrete.
- It can produce findings that influence gate decisions.
- Its likely conflicts with existing roles are named.
- It has initial memory seeds.
- Its registry entry includes `slug`, `name`, `group`, `description`, `applies_to`, `triggers`, `boundary`, `review_focus`, `evidence_needs`, and `conflicts_with`.
- Every `conflicts_with` slug is an existing role or a declared candidate role.
- Its memory seeds are assigned to `Stable Lessons`, `Candidate Improvements`, `Review Patterns`, or `Upgrade Proposals`.

Reject or revise a new role when:

- It duplicates an existing role.
- It is too narrow for one artifact.
- It mixes unrelated perspectives.
- It has no clear evidence needs.
- It cannot affect a decision.
- It exists only because one review lacked context.
- Its registry entry is incomplete.
- Its conflict slugs are unknown or ambiguous.
- Its memory seeds are unclassified.
